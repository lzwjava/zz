The water cycle, also known as the hydrological cycle, describes the continuous movement of water on, above, and below the surface of the Earth. Water evaporates from oceans, lakes, and rivers due to solar energy, rises into the atmosphere as water vapor, cools and condenses to form clouds, and eventually falls back to Earth as precipitation (rain, snow, sleet, or hail). Some precipitation is absorbed into the ground (infiltration), some flows over land as runoff into rivers and lakes, and some is taken up by plants and released back into the atmosphere through transpiration.
